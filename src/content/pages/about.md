George Dawson has lived in Lynchburg since 1980.  Today he paints in both oil and water-based media.  While interested in art throughout his life, his art experience was primarily limited to doodling while sitting in meetings until he retired from his paying job in 2012. 

Since then he has taken art instruction from Lynchburg artists including Ron Boehmer, Purnell Pettyjohn, Pat Harrington, Karen Bowden, Solly Blank, and Rosalie Day White and at the Studio School of the Virginia Museum of Fine Art in Richmond.   National workshop instructors include Nicholas Simmons, John Salminen and recently John MacDonald.   His current interest is in continuing to build his ability with both water-based mediums and oil.  

Dawson paints and exhibits his work at his studio, Studio **One13**, located in Riverviews Art Space, 901 Jefferson Street, Lynchburg, Va. which is open regularly from 5:00 to 8 PM on the first Friday of each month and by appointment with the artist.   Previous recognitions include:  The Academy Center of the Arts National Juried Show, the Katie Vaden Award for Watercolor, Lynchburg Art Festival, September 2016 and again in 2017 , the Bower Center National Juried Show, 2017,  and Judge’s Honorable Mention, 2013 Georgia Morgan Show,  Lynchburg Art Club.
